Center for
Quantum-Enabled Computing

The Center for Quantum-Enabled Computing (C4QEC) aims to pave the way toward verified, energy-efficient, reliable, and scalable computational advantage based on quantum systems

Scope and objectives:

Development of efficient and scalable certification methods for quantum devices.

Exploitation of quantum light–matter coupling for computational speedups in AI.

Investigation of fundamental limits of quantum computing and error correction.

Designing hardware-agnostic quantum algorithms for near-term devices.

Establishment of a world-class center for quantum research and education.

Research Groups

Quantum Information and Certification Group

Group leader: Prof. Remigiusz Augusiak

Focused on creating robust methods to verify and certify quantum states and operations in large-scale systems.

Semiconductor Polariton Group

Group leader: Prof. Michał Matuszewski

Developing ultra-fast and energy-efficient computing systems based on light-matter interactions in semiconductors.

Quantum Computational Supremacy Group

Group leader: Prof. Michał Oszmaniec

Exploring the boundaries of quantum advantage and designing practical algorithms for next-generation hardware.
